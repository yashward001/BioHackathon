Clinician-facing PCOS diagnostic support — Rotterdam criteria, XGBoost ML assessment, and phenotype classification in a single workflow.

01 Exclusion screen

Flags pregnancy, thyroid, hyperprolactinaemia, and endometrial risk before any PCOS interpretation.

02 Rotterdam criteria

Hyperandrogenism, ovulatory dysfunction, and polycystic morphology — each with supporting evidence.

03 ML assessment

XGBoost model (ROC-AUC 0.959) with SHAP-derived phenotype classification across 52 engineered features.

For clinical decision support only — not a substitute for clinician judgement. Results must be reviewed in the context of the full clinical picture.

Clinical protocol

PolyClear overview

Rotterdam-aligned

Clinician-readable PCOS diagnostic support

PolyClear combines an exclusion screen, Rotterdam criteria, and supporting phenotype factors into a transparent output that can be reviewed at the point of care.

Output Verdict + evidence tables + support score
1

Screen exclusions first

Pregnancy, thyroid dysfunction, hyperprolactinaemia, and endometrial risk are surfaced before PCOS interpretation.

2

Apply Rotterdam criteria

Hyperandrogenism, ovulatory dysfunction, and polycystic morphology are each shown with supporting evidence.

3

Explain supporting factors

Metabolic, insulin-resistance, alopecia, and vitamin D signals feed a non-diagnostic explanation score.
